# ADR 076 — `shape:elicit`'s gatekeeper upgrade: diagnose the stall, don't just grill harder

**Status**: accepted
**Date**: 2026-07-14
**Source**: `blueprints/thoughts/2026-07-14-elicit-ecosystem-four-quadrants.md` (the four-quadrant design rulings, court-metaphor positioning) · `blueprints/plans/2026-07-14-elicit-ecosystem.md` (Phase 3 of the implementation plan)
**Phase**: 3 of 4 (`survey` → `probe` → elicit gatekeeper upgrade → behavioral testing); this ADR covers the gatekeeper upgrade to `plugins/shape/skills/elicit/SKILL.md` only.
**Extends**: [ADR-074](074-shape-survey-decision-space-mapping.md) (`survey` — maps a decision space, Phase 1) · [ADR-075](075-shape-probe-ask-reality.md) (`probe` — runs a minimal experiment, Phase 2).

## Context

ADR-074 and ADR-075 built the two missing verbs the four-quadrant analysis named — `survey` for "the world knows, you don't" and `probe` for "nobody knows" — but deliberately left one thing undone in both: **the detection logic that actually routes a stuck elicit volley to either of them.** ADR-074 §Decision 2 states it plainly: "the detection logic lives in elicit (Phase 3, a later ADR)." ADR-075's Consequences repeats the same deferral for `probe`. Until this ADR, `survey` and `probe` existed as summonable verbs with no wiring from the one place a stall is actually visible — mid-grill, inside `elicit`.

The four-quadrant thought (`blueprints/thoughts/2026-07-14-elicit-ecosystem-four-quadrants.md`) frames elicit's necessary upgrade as a change of **identity**, not capability: elicit moves from "convergence engine" to "convergence's gatekeeper" — it doesn't gain a new *move*, it gains the **honesty** to recognize when grilling is the wrong tool and hand off instead of pretending one more fork will help.

| Knowledge state | Solved by | Verb |
|---|---|---|
| I know I know | Draw it out and align | `shape:elicit` (existing engine, unchanged) |
| I know I don't know (the world knows) | Map → fill the gap → re-align | `shape:survey` (ADR-074) |
| I don't know I don't know (the world knows) | elicit detects it mid-grill → offers survey | **elicit's gatekeeper role (this ADR)** |
| Nobody knows | Design a minimal experiment | `shape:probe` (ADR-075) — **also offered by elicit's gatekeeper role (this ADR)** |

This ADR completes the routing for the bottom two rows: elicit is the only verb in the room when either kind of stall surfaces, so it is the only place the detection can live.

## Decision

1. **Detection lives in elicit because it is the only verb present when the stall happens — the invocation paradox, resolved from elicit's side.** ADR-074 §2 already named the paradox for `survey` alone: nobody spontaneously summons a skill to reveal their own blind spot. The same paradox applies to `probe` — nobody summons "tell me nobody knows this" mid-argument, they just keep arguing. The only resolution that doesn't require a second standing engine watching every conversation is to park detection inside the skill that's *already running* when the stall occurs. That skill is elicit. This ADR is the other half of ADR-074's deferred decision, now wired.
2. **The engine is untouched — gatekeeping is a new duty at the STALL point, not a new move.** The 6 moves, the Core (weight-adaptive exit), the stance (react-not-author), and diagnostic mode are byte-identical after this change. A parallel "diagnose-the-stall" skill, or a rewritten move set, would be the N+1 anti-pattern this family has refused at every turn (`shape:position` borrows elicit/mockup/first-principles *by protocol* rather than copying their engines, ADR-008; diagnostic mode lives *inside* elicit rather than a separate `diagnose` skill, for the identical reason). Gatekeeping is scoped narrowly: it fires only at the moment a volley stalls, diagnoses which of three stalls it is, and for two of the three it stops and offers — it never replaces or reorders the existing 6 moves.
3. **Three-way stall diagnosis, not a binary "stuck vs not stuck."** A stalled volley (the user can't pick, forks keep floating, the same layer re-argues) can mean three structurally different things, and only one of them is fixed by grilling harder:
   - **(1) The answer is in the user's head but fuzzy** — the ordinary shape of a hard convergence. Keep grilling; nothing changes here.
   - **(2) The user is missing terrain the world has** — a blind spot, possibly one they don't know they have. Grilling a blank spot produces nothing to draw out — friction against a void just burns turns. The honest move is to **stop** and offer `/shape:survey`.
   - **(3) Nobody knows** — the fork rests on an unmeasured fact, not an opinion or a documented answer. More argument produces confident-sounding noise, not evidence. Offer `/shape:probe`.

   Collapsing (2) and (3) into one generic "I'm offering you something else" would blur exactly the distinction the four-quadrant map exists to preserve — a missing map and a missing measurement are fixed by different verbs, and conflating them would route the user to the wrong one half the time.
4. **The court metaphor is the positioning statement, stated once, in the `SKILL.md` itself (not just in the source thought).** elicit is the court: background unclear → call the expert witness (`survey`); facts disputed → order the forensic test (`probe`); **the ruling itself never leaves the court** — survey and probe supply evidence, elicit converges it. This is the line that keeps `survey`/`probe` from drifting into deciding anything themselves — their SKILL.md files (ADR-074 §Decision 1, ADR-075 §Decision 1) already refuse to rule; this ADR is elicit's side of the same boundary, stated from the seat that actually holds the gavel.
5. **Offers, never calls — guarded, one-shot, degrade gracefully if `survey`/`probe` aren't installed.** Same law as every other cross-skill hand-off in this family (ADR-007/015, and every offer branch already in elicit's Protocol step 6). The gatekeeper upgrade adds two new offer branches that fire **mid-grill, at the stall point** — a new timing, distinct from the existing offer branches which all fire **after** a thought lands. This distinction is stated explicitly in the `SKILL.md` so the two sets of branches aren't read as interchangeable.
6. **After survey or probe returns, the volley resumes** — not a fresh elicit session, not a handoff that ends the grill. The user comes back with a filled-in fork (evidence from survey's diff, or probe's verdict) and elicit's Protocol step 3 (Volley) continues from there, now grilling a fork that actually has ground under it.
7. **This completes the ecosystem's routing.** All four quadrants of the knowledge map now have an owning verb: quadrant 1 was always elicit's; quadrants 2 and 4 got new verbs in ADR-074/075; quadrant 3 (the unknown-unknown) is resolved by this ADR's detection logic living in elicit, the only place it can be seen.

## Why

- **Parking detection in the one skill already in the room is not a workaround — it's the only structurally sound placement.** A separate "stall watcher" skill would need to either run continuously (violating the summoned-not-automatic law that governs this entire family) or be summoned by the user at the exact moment they don't know they need it (the paradox ADR-074 already named and rejected as unsolvable any other way). Elicit already sees every turn of the volley; it is the cheapest and only honest place to look for the stall.
- **Not touching the engine is what keeps this a *duty*, not a *feature*.** If gatekeeping required new moves or a reordered protocol, it would compete with the weight-adaptive exit for elicit's attention on every turn — diluting the one non-negotiable this skill has (Core: stop the instant the user snaps to a principle). Scoping gatekeeping to the stall point only, with the existing 6 moves otherwise unchanged, keeps the addition a narrow honesty check rather than a second engine bolted onto the first.
- **The three-way split matters because the two "give up and hand off" cases are not the same failure.** A missing axis of the terrain and an unmeasured fact about reality call for different verbs doing different work (mapping vs. measuring) — a gatekeeper that couldn't tell them apart would just be a coin flip between `survey` and `probe`, which is worse than not gatekeeping at all, because it would route confidently to the wrong tool.
- **The court metaphor earns its place in the `SKILL.md` body, not just the design doc, because "offers, never calls" needs the sharpest possible one-line justification a reader can carry forward.** Every other skill in this family restates its own rules self-containedly (repo convention: self-contained SKILL.md); the metaphor is the cheapest way to make "why does elicit hand off instead of ruling" legible without re-deriving the whole four-quadrant argument inline.
- **Completing all four quadrants closes a design gap that was explicitly left open twice.** Both ADR-074 and ADR-075 named this exact deferral in their own Consequences sections — this ADR isn't introducing new scope, it's discharging a debt both prior ADRs already flagged.
